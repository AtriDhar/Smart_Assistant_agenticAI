REVISION TIPS & STUDY STRATEGIES
B.Tech CSCE Program | KIIT University
Compiled for the 2023-2027 Batch
============================================================

1. GENERAL STUDY PHILOSOPHY

The goal of studying is not to memorize — it is to understand deeply enough
to apply the knowledge to new problems. Exams in engineering test understanding,
not rote learning. Use these strategies to study smarter, not harder.

2. THE POMODORO TECHNIQUE (Recommended for all subjects)

Work in focused 25-minute sessions with 5-minute breaks.
  - Session 1: 25 min study → 5 min break
  - Session 2: 25 min study → 5 min break
  - Session 3: 25 min study → 5 min break
  - Session 4: 25 min study → 20 min long break (Repeat cycle)

Best for: Coding practice, problem-solving, reading lecture notes.
Tool: Forest App, Tomato Timer, or a simple phone timer.

3. ACTIVE RECALL (Most powerful study technique)

Instead of re-reading notes, test yourself:
  - After reading a chapter, close the book and write everything you remember.
  - Use flashcards (digital: Anki app) for definitions, algorithms, theorems.
  - Solve previous year question papers WITHOUT looking at solutions first.
  - Explain concepts aloud to yourself (Feynman Technique).
  - Teach the concept to a friend — if you can teach it, you know it.

4. SPACED REPETITION

Review material at increasing intervals to commit it to long-term memory.
Schedule:
  Day 1: Learn new material.
  Day 2: Quick 15-min review.
  Day 5: Another 10-min review.
  Day 14: Quick review.
  Day 30: Final revision before exam.

Use the Anki app (free) which automates spaced repetition for flashcards.

5. SUBJECT-SPECIFIC REVISION TIPS

DATA STRUCTURES & ALGORITHMS:
  - Practice writing code by hand (for viva and exams).
  - Solve at least 5 problems per data structure on LeetCode or HackerRank.
  - Draw diagrams for tree rotations (AVL, B-Tree), graph traversals.
  - Memorize time and space complexities of all algorithms as a table.
  - Important: Trace through Dijkstra's, BFS, DFS step-by-step with examples.
  - For sorting: Know which algorithms are stable and which are in-place.
  - Recommended PYQs: Focus on recurrence relations and algorithm design.

OPERATING SYSTEMS:
  - Draw and memorize the 5-state process model diagram.
  - Practice CPU scheduling Gantt charts (FCFS, SJF, RR with given data).
  - Memorize all 4 Coffman conditions for deadlock; practice Banker's Algorithm.
  - For memory management: work through paging address translation numericals.
  - Know LRU, FIFO, Optimal page replacement algorithms with frame examples.
  - Practice: Given a reference string, compute page faults with 3 frames.

DATABASE MANAGEMENT SYSTEMS:
  - Write SQL queries every day — use SQLZoo.net or W3Schools SQL practice.
  - Draw ER diagrams from scratch for common scenarios (Library, Hospital, Bank).
  - Normalize a given relation to BCNF step-by-step for exam practice.
  - Memorize all relational algebra operators with syntax and examples.
  - Understand 2PL with lock compatibility matrix; practice serializability.

COMPUTER NETWORKS:
  - Draw the OSI model from memory; map every protocol to its layer.
  - Practice subnetting: given IP/prefix, find network address, broadcast, host range.
  - Trace a packet from source to destination through all layers.
  - Know port numbers for major protocols; they appear in MCQs.
  - For routing: trace Dijkstra's algorithm on a small network graph.
  - CRC calculation: practice polynomial division problems.

SOFTWARE ENGINEERING:
  - Draw SDLC model diagrams (Waterfall V-shape, Spiral quadrants).
  - Know Agile vs Waterfall pros and cons for comparison questions.
  - Draw UML class and sequence diagrams for given scenarios.
  - Practice: Write a mini SRS for a simple system (Library management, ATM).
  - COCOMO: Memorize basic formula and modes.

6. EXAM PREPARATION STRATEGY

4 WEEKS BEFORE EXAM:
  - Cover all 5 modules at least once.
  - Complete all assignments and lab exercises.
  - Make short notes (1 page per module with key points).

2 WEEKS BEFORE EXAM:
  - Solve last 3–5 years of previous year questions (PYQs).
  - Identify repeated themes and high-weightage topics.
  - Do 2–3 mock tests under exam conditions (3 hours, no distractions).

1 WEEK BEFORE EXAM:
  - Only review one-page notes and flashcards.
  - Do NOT start new topics. Revise what you know.
  - Focus on algorithm problems and numerical problems (guaranteed marks).
  - Get 8 hours of sleep every night.

DAY BEFORE EXAM:
  - Light revision only (1–2 hours).
  - Read through one-page formula/concept sheets.
  - Lay out your exam kit the night before.
  - Sleep by 10:30 PM.

EXAM DAY:
  - Wake up 2 hours before reporting time; eat a light breakfast.
  - Carry: Hall ticket + KIIT ID card + pens (3 minimum) + non-programmable calculator.
  - Read all questions before starting; attempt high-confidence questions first.
  - For 10-mark questions: write headings, points, diagrams — examiner rewards structure.
  - Budget time: 3-hour exam → 3 minutes per mark.

7. MENTAL HEALTH & WELLBEING

- Take care of sleep: 7–8 hours per night is non-negotiable.
- During stress periods: 20-min walks, deep breathing, talking to friends.
- KIIT has a Student Wellness Centre with counsellors (free of charge).
- Study in groups for difficult topics; isolation makes hard topics harder.
- Avoid back-to-back all-nighters; they reduce retention significantly.
- Use university library (open until 10 PM on weekdays).
