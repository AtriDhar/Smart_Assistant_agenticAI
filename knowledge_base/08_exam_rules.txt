2. MID SEMESTER EXAMINATION (MSE)

- Commonly called Mid Sem or Mid Term Exam.
- Conducted around Week 7–8 of each semester.
- Duration: 1.5 hours (90 minutes).
- Syllabus: Modules 1 and 2 of each subject (approximately 40% of full syllabus).
- Maximum Marks: 20 per subject.
- Question Pattern:
  * Short-answer questions (2 marks × 5 = 10 marks)
  * Long-answer questions (5 marks × 2 = 10 marks)
- No hall ticket required for Mid Sem; regular student ID card is sufficient.
- Venue: Declared on the university ERP portal 3 days prior.

3. END SEMESTER EXAMINATION (ESE)

- Conducted after Week 15–16 (at the end of semester).
- Practical exams: Last two weeks of the semester.
- Duration: 3 hours (180 minutes).
- Syllabus: Entire semester syllabus (all 5 modules).
- Maximum Marks: 60 per subject.
- Question Pattern (standard format):
  * Section A: 2 marks × 10 = 20 marks (short answers, compulsory)
  * Section B: 5 marks × 4 = 20 marks (choose 4 from 6)
  * Section C: 10 marks × 2 = 20 marks (choose 2 from 4; long answers)
- HALL TICKET: Mandatory for End Semester Exam.
  - Hall ticket issued via university ERP portal (erp.kiit.ac.in).
  - Available 5–7 days before exam.
  - Students must verify their details; errors must be reported to CoE office immediately.
  - Hall ticket must be presented along with KIIT ID card before entering exam hall.
  - No hall ticket = Not allowed to appear in exam. No exceptions.

4. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA FOR END SEMESTER EXAM

ATTENDANCE REQUIREMENT:
- Minimum 75% attendance in each subject is compulsory.
- Students with attendance below 75% are debarred from ESE in that subject.
- Debarred students automatically get 'F' grade and must repeat the subject.

MEDICAL CONDONATION:
  - Students between 65%–75% with valid medical proof may apply for condonation.
  - Application to Dean (Academic Affairs) with original medical certificate.
  - Maximum condonation: 10% can be condoned (so effective minimum = 65%).
  - Condonation NOT applicable if attendance is below 65%.

DUES/LIBRARY CLEARANCE:
  - All library dues, hostel dues, and fee arrears must be cleared.
  - Students with pending dues may not receive hall ticket.

BACK PAPER (SUPPLEMENTARY):
  - Students who fail (< 40 in ESE or < 45 overall) can appear in supplementary.
  - Supplementary Exam: Held within 4–6 weeks of result declaration.
  - Maximum marks in supplementary ESE = 60 (no override of internal marks).

5. EXAMINATION CONDUCT RULES

During Examination:
  - Students must arrive at exam hall at least 15 minutes before start time.
  - Late entry: Allowed up to 30 minutes; no extra time given.
  - Entry NOT allowed after 30 minutes from start.
  - Students may leave only after 1 hour from exam start.

Prohibited Items:
  - Mobile phones, smartwatches, Bluetooth devices — STRICTLY PROHIBITED.
  - Programmable calculators not allowed (simple non-programmable only).
  - Chits, handwritten notes of any kind.
  - Electronic devices including earphones, tablets.
  - Bags must be kept outside; only essential stationery allowed inside.

Seating:
  - Assigned seats as per hall ticket; changing seats is not permitted.
  - Students must sit as per room allotment on hall ticket.

Answer Sheet:
  - Write Roll Number, Subject Code, Date on every answer sheet.
  - Additional sheets must be tagged with proper serial numbers.
  - Choose questions carefully; questions once attempted will be evaluated.

Unfair Means (UFM):
  - Possession of unauthorized material = UFM case; subject fails.
  - Copying or helping to copy = both students penalized.
  - UFM cases go to the Examination Disciplinary Committee.
  - Penalty: F grade in the subject + possible suspension (repeat offenses).

6. RESULT DECLARATION

- Results declared within 30–45 days of ESE completion.
- Available on ERP portal and KIIT official website.
- Grade card issued after result.
- Revaluation (Scrutiny):
  - Students can apply for scrutiny within 7 days of result declaration.
  - Fee: Rs. 500 per subject.
  - Rechecking involves totaling marks; not a re-evaluation of answers.

8. SPECIAL PROVISIONS

Medical Emergency During Exam:
  - If a student falls ill during exam, invigilator must be informed.
  - Partial exam with valid medical reason → Medical Exam (ME) consideration.

COVID/Force Majeure:
  - University follows government guidelines; makeup exams as per UGC norms.

Students with Disabilities:
  - Eligible for extra time (up to 1 hour) and scribe assistance.
  - Apply to Dean's office at semester start with disability certificate.
