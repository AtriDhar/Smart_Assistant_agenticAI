2. GRADING SCALE (10-Point System)

KIIT University follows a 10-point absolute grading system.

Grade  Grade Point  Marks Range (out of 100)  Performance
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  O       10           90 – 100               Outstanding
  E        9           80 – 89                Excellent
  A        8           70 – 79                Very Good
  B        7           60 – 69                Good
  C        6           50 – 59                Average
  D        5           45 – 49                Satisfactory
  F        0           Below 45               Fail

NOTE: 'F' grade = FAIL. The subject must be repeated or supplementary exam taken.

3. PASSING CRITERIA

To pass a subject, a student must satisfy BOTH:
  Condition 1: Minimum 40 marks out of 60 in End Semester Examination.
  Condition 2: Minimum 45 marks out of 100 in total (Internal + ESE).

Failing Condition 1 alone → FAIL (even if overall ≥ 45).
Failing Condition 2 alone → FAIL (even if ESE ≥ 40).
Both conditions must be satisfied simultaneously.

For Lab subjects: Minimum 20 out of 50 required to pass.

4. SGPA CALCULATION (Semester Grade Point Average)

Formula: SGPA = Σ(Credit_i × GradePoint_i) / Σ(Credit_i)

Where i runs over all subjects in the semester.

Example (Semester 3):
Subject           Credits  Grade  GP   Credits × GP
Data Structures      4       B    7      28
Operating Systems    3       A    8      24
DBMS                 3       E    9      27
Discrete Math        3       B    7      21
DS Lab               2       O   10      20
OS Lab               2       A    8      16
Total Credits       17                  136

SGPA = 136 / 17 = 8.0

5. CGPA CALCULATION (Cumulative Grade Point Average)

CGPA = Σ(Credits × GradePoints for ALL completed semesters) / Total Credits completed.

CGPA is calculated after each semester and updated on the grade card.

CGPA to Percentage Conversion (as per KIIT norms):
  Percentage = (CGPA − 0.5) × 10
  Example: CGPA 8.0 → (8.0 − 0.5) × 10 = 75%

6. GRADE CATEGORIES FOR PLACEMENT & SCHOLARSHIPS

  CGPA ≥ 9.0: Chancellor's Award eligible; top merit scholarship.
  CGPA ≥ 8.5: Dean's List; merit scholarship eligible.
  CGPA ≥ 7.5: Distinction in final certificate.
  CGPA ≥ 6.5: Eligible for most campus placement opportunities.
  CGPA ≥ 5.0: Eligible for supplementary advantages; some roles may require waiver.
  CGPA < 5.0: Academic probation; counseling mandatory.

7. BACK PAPER POLICY

If a student fails a subject (gets F grade):
  a. Supplementary Exam: Held within 4–6 weeks of result.
     - Internal marks of original semester retained.
     - Only external exam (60 marks) re-taken.
     - Maximum grade achievable in supplementary: D (5 grade points).
  b. If supplementary also failed: Subject appears as back paper in a future semester.
  c. Maximum back papers allowed: 4 per semester; if exceeded, student may be detained.

IMPORTANT: Even if a student passes via supplementary, the 'F' grade does NOT appear on the transcript if cleared. The new grade replaces it.

8. GRACE MARKS POLICY

  - Up to 5 grace marks may be awarded in a subject at the university's discretion.
  - Grace marks may help push a student from 'F' to 'D' (44 → 45 with grace = pass).
  - Grace marks are awarded sparingly; NOT guaranteed.
  - Grace marks are typically applied to End Semester Exam marks only.

9. HONORS / DISTINCTION CRITERIA

For Final Degree Classification:
  - First Class with Distinction: CGPA ≥ 7.5
  - First Class: CGPA ≥ 6.5
  - Second Class: CGPA ≥ 5.5
  - Pass Class: CGPA ≥ 5.0

  Note: No back papers in final two semesters required for Distinction.

10. INTERNAL MARKS TRANSPARENCY

Each faculty must:
  - Display internal marks on the Department Notice Board before ESE.
  - Provide opportunity for students to review their Mid Sem answer scripts.
  - Students can dispute internal marks within 7 days of display.
  - Disputes escalated to HOD if faculty cannot resolve.

11. SUBJECT-SPECIFIC MARK BREAKUP (Mid Sem Exam Pattern)

Mid Sem Exam (20 Marks):
  Section A: Short Questions (4 × 2 = 8 marks): All compulsory.
  Section B: Medium Questions (choose 2 from 3, 6 marks each = 12 marks).

End Sem Exam (60 Marks):
  Section A: Very Short / MCQ (10 × 2 = 20 marks): All compulsory.
  Section B: Medium (choose 4 from 6, 5 marks each = 20 marks).
  Section C: Long Answers (choose 2 from 4, 10 marks each = 20 marks).
